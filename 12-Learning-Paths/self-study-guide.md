# Self-Study Guide for Learning Marathi

A flexible, customizable guide for independent learners. Design your own learning path based on your goals, available time, and interests.

## 🎯 Getting Started

### Step 1: Assess Your Level

**Complete Beginner:**
- No previous exposure to Marathi
- Cannot read Devanagari script
- Start with [Alphabet section](../01-Alphabet/)

**Basic (1-3 months study):**
- Can read Devanagari
- Know 50-100 words
- Understand basic grammar
- Continue with [Grammar](../03-Grammar/) and [Vocabulary](../04-Vocabulary/)

**Intermediate (6+ months study):**
- Comfortable with basic conversations
- Know 300+ words
- Understand most grammar
- Focus on [Reading](../06-Reading/), [Writing](../07-Writing/), [Culture](../08-Culture/)

---

### Step 2: Define Your Goals

**Why are you learning Marathi?**

| Goal | Focus Areas | Priority Resources |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Travel to Maharashtra | Conversations, practical vocabulary | [05-Conversations](../05-Conversations/), [04-Vocabulary](../04-Vocabulary/) |
| Heritage/family connection | Speaking, cultural context | [05-Conversations](../05-Conversations/), [08-Culture](../08-Culture/) |
| Work/business | Professional vocabulary, formal language | Grammar, Writing, Vocabulary |
| Academic interest | Reading, writing, grammar | All sections equally |
| Enjoyment/hobby | Balanced approach | Your choice! |

---

### Step 3: Set Your Schedule

**Minimum Effective Dose:** 15-20 minutes daily
**Recommended:** 30-60 minutes daily
**Intensive:** 1-2 hours daily

Choose a realistic schedule you can maintain:

**Morning Person:**
- 20 min vocabulary review with coffee
- 15 min audio during commute
- 15 min writing practice in evening

**Evening Person:**
- 30 min study session after dinner
- 15 min review before bed

**Weekend Warrior:**
- 1-2 hours on weekend days
- 10 min daily maintenance

**Resources:**
- [YouTube Channels](../09-Resources/youtube.md)
- [Movies](../09-Resources/movies.md)
- Audio from conversation sections

**Practice Methods:**
1. **Passive Listening:** Play Marathi audio while doing other tasks
2. **Active Listening:** Focus and try to understand every word
3. **Shadowing:** Repeat immediately after hearing
4. **Dictation:** Write what you hear

**Progression:**
- Slow, simple audio (children's content)
- Normal speed conversations
- Native speaker speed
- Media with regional accents

## 🔧 Study Tools & Techniques

### Spaced Repetition

**Review Schedule:**
| Review | Timing |
|--------|--------|
| 1st review | 1 day after learning |
| 2nd review | 3 days after |
| 3rd review | 1 week after |
| 4th review | 2 weeks after |
| 5th review | 1 month after |

### The Pomodoro Technique

1. Study for 25 minutes
2. Take 5-minute break
3. Repeat 4 times
4. Take 15-30 minute break

### Active Recall

Instead of re-reading:
- Close the book and recall
- Write from memory
- Explain to someone else
- Test yourself frequently

### Interleaving

Mix different topics in each session:
- 10 min grammar + 10 min vocabulary + 10 min reading
Better than long blocks of single topics.

## 💡 Tips for Success

### Do's:
- ✅ Study consistently, even if briefly
- ✅ Practice speaking from day one
- ✅ Make mistakes and learn from them
- ✅ Immerse yourself in Marathi media
- ✅ Find study partners or communities
- ✅ Celebrate small wins
- ✅ Review regularly

### Don'ts:
- ❌ Skip the script - it's essential
- ❌ Focus only on reading/writing
- ❌ Compare yourself to others
- ❌ Give up when progress seems slow
- ❌ Study for hours once a week
- ❌ Move on before understanding basics

## 🆘 Troubleshooting Common Problems

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Can't remember vocabulary | Use flashcards, spaced repetition, context |
| Grammar is confusing | Focus on one topic at a time, use examples |
| Pronunciation is hard | Listen more, record yourself, use mirror |
| No one to practice with | Talk to yourself, find online partners |
| Losing motivation | Set smaller goals, find interesting content |
| Progress seems slow | Track progress, celebrate small wins |
| Don't understand native speakers | Start with slower content, use subtitles |
