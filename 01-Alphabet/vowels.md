# Marathi Vowels | मराठी स्वर

## Learning Objectives | शिक्षण उद्दिष्ट्ये

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

- **Identify** all 12 Marathi vowels in written form 
- **Pronounce** each vowel correctly with proper length
- **Write** each vowel character with correct stroke order
- **Recognize** the diacritic forms of vowels
- **Distinguish** between short and long vowels
- **Read** simple words using vowels

---

## Introduction | परिचय

Vowels (स्वर - svara) are the foundation of any Indian language. In Marathi, vowels are sounds that are produced without any obstruction in the vocal tract. They form the core of every syllable.

### Types of Vowels | स्वरांचे प्रकार

| Type | Marathi | Examples |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Short vowels** | ह्रस्व स्वर | अ, इ, उ |
| **Long vowels** | दीर्घ स्वर | आ, ई, ऊ, ए, ऐ, ओ, औ |
| **Vocalic** | वर्णांतर | ऋ |
| **Nasalized** | अनुस्वार/विसर्ग | अं, अः |

## Detailed Vowel Guide | तपशीलवार स्वर मार्गदर्शक

### 1. अ (a) - Short 'a'

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Devanagari** | अ |
| **Transliteration** | a |
| **IPA** | /ə/ |
| **Type** | Short (ह्रस्व) |
| **Diacritic** | (inherent in consonants) |

**How to Pronounce:**
- Like the 'a' in English "a**b**out" or "**u**pon"
- This is a neutral, short vowel
- The mouth is relaxed and slightly open
- The tongue rests in a neutral position

**Example Words:**

| Marathi | Transliteration | Meaning | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|---------|-------|
| अकबर | akbar | Akbar (name) | Initial position |
| नमस्कार | namaskār | Hello/Greetings | Middle position |
| कर | kar | do (verb) | With consonant |
| अज | aj | today | Initial position |

**Practice Words:**
- अनंत (anant) - infinite
- अर्थ (arth) - meaning
- बट (baṭa) - button
- पग (paga) - step

---

### 2. आ (ā) - Long 'a'

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Devanagari** | आ |
| **Transliteration** | ā |
| **IPA** | /ɑː/ |
| **Type** | Long (दीर्घ) |
| **Diacritic** | ा (placed after consonant) |

**How to Pronounce:**
- Like the 'a' in English "f**a**ther" or "c**a**r"
- Hold the sound longer than अ
- Open your mouth wider than for अ
- The sound comes from the back of the throat

**Example Words:**

| Marathi | Transliteration | Meaning | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|---------|-------|
| आम | ām | mango | Initial position |
| बाप | bāp | father | With consonant |
| आकाश | ākāś | sky | Initial position |
| जाणे | jāṇe | to go | Middle position |

**Practice Words:**
- आनंद (ānand) - happiness
- माता (mātā) - mother
- पान (pān) - leaf
- दार (dār) - door

**⚠️ Important Distinction:**
```
अ (a) vs आ (ā)
----------------
अज (aj) = today    vs    आज (āj) = today (alternate)
कर (kar) = do      vs    कार (kār) = car
नम (nam) = name    vs    नाम (nām) = name
```

### 3. इ (i) - Short 'i'

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Devanagari** | इ |
| **Transliteration** | i |
| **IPA** | /i/ |
| **Type** | Short (ह्रस्व) |
| **Diacritic** | ि (placed before consonant) |

**How to Pronounce:**
- Like the 'i' in English "b**i**t" or "s**i**t"
- Short and quick
- Lips spread slightly
- Tongue positioned high and forward

**Example Words:**

| Marathi | Transliteration | Meaning | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|---------|-------|
| इमारत | imārat | building | Initial position |
| दिन | din | day | With consonant |
| बिल | bil | bill | With consonant |
| इतर | itar | other | Initial position |

**Practice Words:**
- इतिहास (itihās) - history
- दिवस (divas) - day
- मित्र (mitr) - friend
- निशाणी (niśāṇī) - sign/mark

**Note on Diacritic Placement:**
The diacritic ि appears **before** the consonant it modifies:
- क + ि = कि (ki) ← The ि comes before क in writing
- म + ि = मि (mi)

### 4. ई (ī) - Long 'i'

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Devanagari** | ई |
| **Transliteration** | ī |
| **IPA** | /iː/ |
| **Type** | Long (दीर्घ) |
| **Diacritic** | ी (placed after consonant) |

**How to Pronounce:**
- Like the 'ee' in English "s**ee**" or "tr**ee**"
- Hold the sound twice as long as इ
- Lips spread more
- Tongue high and forward, held longer

**Example Words:**

| Marathi | Transliteration | Meaning | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|---------|-------|
| ईश्वर | īśvar | God | Initial position |
| दीन | dīn | poor/humble | With consonant |
| मीन | mīn | fish | With consonant |
| ईड | īḍ | pin | Initial position |

**Practice Words:**
- ईट (īṭ) - brick
- दीवाळी (dīvāḷī) - Diwali festival
- पीठ (pīṭh) - flour
- नील (nīl) - blue

**⚠️ Important Distinction:**
```
इ (i) vs ई (ī)
----------------
मित्र (mitr) = friend    vs    मीत्र (mītr) = friend (variant)
दिन (din) = day          vs    दीन (dīn) = poor/humble
सिंह (siṃh) = lion       vs    सींह (sīṃh) = lion (variant)
```

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Devanagari** | उ |
| **Transliteration** | u |
| **IPA** | /u/ |

### 6. ऊ (ū) - Long 'u'

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Devanagari** | ऊ |
| **Transliteration** | ū |
| **IPA** | /uː/ |
| **Type** | Long (दीर्घ) |
| **Diacritic** | ू (placed below consonant) |

**How to Pronounce:**
- Like the 'oo' in English "m**oo**n" or "sp**oo**n"
- Hold the sound twice as long as उ
- Lips rounded more
- Tongue high and back, held longer

**Example Words:**

| Marathi | Transliteration | Meaning | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|---------|-------|
| ऊन | ūn | sunshine | Initial position |
| भूमी | bhūmī | earth/land | With consonant |
| दूध | dūdh | milk | With consonant |
| ऊर्जा | ūrjā | energy | Initial position |

**Practice Words:**
- ऊस (ūs) - sugarcane
- सूर्य (sūrya) - sun
- भूक (bhūk) - hunger
- मूल (mūl) - child/root

**⚠️ Important Distinction:**
```
उ (u) vs ऊ (ū)
----------------
गुण (guṇ) = quality       vs    गूण (gūṇ) = thread
सुधा (sudhā) = nectar     vs    सूधा (sūdhā) = direction
मुक्त (mukt) = free       vs    मूक (mūk) = dumb/silent
```

### 7. ऋ (ṛ) - Vocalic 'r'

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Devanagari** | ऋ |
| **Transliteration** | ṛ |
| **IPA** | /ɻ̩/ |
| **Type** | Vocalic (वर्णांतर) |
| **Diacritic** | ृ (placed below consonant) |

**How to Pronounce:**
- Has no exact equivalent in English
- Approximate: Try saying "r**i**ght" without fully pronouncing the 'i'
- The tongue tip curls back while producing the sound
- A combination of 'r' quality with vowel resonance

**Note:** This vowel is rare in everyday Marathi and appears mainly in Sanskrit loanwords.

**Example Words:**

| Marathi | Transliteration | Meaning | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|---------|-------|
| ऋषि | ṛṣi | sage/saint | Sanskrit loanword |
| ऋतू | ṛtū | season | Sanskrit loanword |
| पृथ्वी | pṛthvī | earth | With consonant |
| धृष्ट | dhṛṣṭ | bold/audacious | Sanskrit loanword |

**Practice Words:**
- ऋण (ṛṇa) - debt
- पृथक (pṛthak) - separate
- मृत्यु (mṛtyu) - death
- कृपा (kṛpā) - grace

### 8. ए (e) - Long 'e'

| Property | Value |

**Practice Words:**

- देव (dev) - god
- पेटी (peṭī) - box/chest
- रेखा (rekhā) - line

### 9. ऐ (ai) - Diphthong 'ai'

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Devanagari** | ऐ |
| **Transliteration** | ai |
| **IPA** | /əi/ |
| **Type** | Diphthong (संधिस्वर) |

| ऐन | ain | very/same moment | Initial position |

**Practice Words:**
- ऐश्वर्य (aiśvarya) - prosperity
- कैद (kaid) - prisoner

- सैनिक (sainik) - soldier

### 12. अं (aṃ) - Anusvara (Nasal)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Devanagari** | अं |
| **Transliteration** | aṃ / ã |
| **IPA** | /ə̃/ |
| **Type** | Nasal (अनुस्वार) |
| **Diacritic** | ं (dot above consonant) |

**How to Pronounce:**
- A nasalized vowel
- The air flows through the nose
- Like the 'um' in "rhyth**m**" but without closing lips
- The exact pronunciation depends on the following consonant

**Pronunciation Rules:**
- Before क, ख, ग, घ → nasal 'a' with back of tongue raised (aŋ)
- Before च, छ, ज, झ → nasal 'a' with tongue behind teeth (aɲ)
- Before ट, ठ, ड, ढ → nasal 'a' with curled tongue (aɳ)
- Before त, थ, द, ध → dental nasal (an)
- Before प, फ, ब, भ → like 'um' in English (am)

**Example Words:**

| Marathi | Transliteration | Meaning | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|---------|-------|
| अंग | aṅg | body | Before ग (velar) |
| अंजीर | añjīr | fig | Before ज (palatal) |
| अंडा | aṇḍā | egg | Before ड (retroflex) |
| अंत | ant | end | Before त (dental) |
| अंबा | ambā | mango | Before ब (labial) |

**Practice Words:**
- अंक (aṅk) - number
- अंश (aṃś) - part
- संग (saṅg) - company
- हिंसा (hiṃsā) - violence

### 13. अः (aḥ) - Visarga

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Devanagari** | अः |
| **Transliteration** | aḥ |
| **IPA** | /əɦ/ |
| **Type** | Visarga (विसर्ग) |
| **Diacritic** |ः (two dots to the right) |

**How to Pronounce:**
- Pronounced as a short 'a' followed by a breathy 'h'
- Like saying "uh-huh" with emphasis on the 'h'
- Creates a slight pause or emphasis
- Most common at the end of words

**Example Words:**

| Marathi | Transliteration | Meaning | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|---------|-------|
| दुःख | duḥkh | sorrow | Middle position |
| अः | aḥ | exclamation | Standalone |
| पुनः | punaḥ | again | Word final |
| अतिथिः | atithiḥ | guest | Sanskrit form |

**Practice Words:**
- नमः (namaḥ) - salutation
- दयालुः (dayāluḥ) - kind person
- बाहुः (bāhuḥ) - arm

**Note:** Visarga is less common in everyday Marathi compared to Sanskrit. In modern Marathi, it often changes to other sounds or is dropped.

## Diacritic Forms Summary | मात्रा सारांश

When vowels combine with consonants, they appear as diacritical marks called **मात्रा (mātrā)**:

| Independent | Diacritic | Name | Position |
|-------------|-----------|------|----------|
| अ (a) | — | (inherent) | — |
| आ (ā) | ा | आ-मात्रा | After consonant |
| इ (i) | ि | इ-मात्रा | Before consonant |
| ई (ī) | ी | ई-मात्रा | After consonant |
| उ (u) | ु | उ-मात्रा | Below consonant |
| ऊ (ū) | ू | ऊ-मात्रा | Below consonant |
| ऋ (ṛ) | ृ | ऋ-मात्रा | Below consonant |
| ए (e) | े | ए-मात्रा | Above consonant |
| ऐ (ai) | ै | ऐ-मात्रा | Above consonant |
| ओ (o) | ो | ओ-मात्रा | Above consonant |
| औ (au) | ौ | औ-मात्रा | Above consonant |

### Example with क (ka):

| Combined | Form | Transliteration |
|----------|------|-----------------|
| क + अ | क | ka |
| क + आ | का | kā |
| क + इ | कि | ki |
| क + ई | की | kī |
| क + उ | कु | ku |
| क + ऊ | कू | kū |
| क + ऋ | कृ | kṛ |
| क + ए | के | ke |
| क + ऐ | कै | kai |
| क + ओ | को | ko |
| क + औ | कौ | kau |

## Short vs Long Vowels | ह्रस्व व दीर्घ स्वर

Understanding vowel length is crucial in Marathi:

### Pairs of Short and Long Vowels:

| Short | Long | Examples |
|-------|------|----------|
| अ (a) | आ (ā) | अज (aj) ≠ आज (āj) |
| इ (i) | ई (ī) | मित्र (mitr) ≠ मीत्र (mītr) |
| उ (u) | ऊ (ū) | मुक्त (mukt) ≠ मूक (mūk) |

### Vowel Length Changes Meaning:

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| कर (kar) | do (verb) |
| कार (kār) | car |
| हिन (hin) | small |
| हीन (hīn) | devoid of |
| सुख (sukh) | happiness |
| सूख (sūkh) | dry |

## Cultural Note | सांस्कृतिक टिप्पणी

### Om (ॐ) - The Primordial Sound

The sacred syllable **ॐ (Oṃ)** is formed by combining three sounds:

- **अ (a)** - Beginning
- **उ (u)** - Middle  
- **म (m)** - End

Together, they represent the past, present, and future - all that exists. This shows how fundamental vowels are to Indian spiritual thought.

### Sanskrit's Influence

Many Marathi vowels come from Sanskrit. The Sanskrit alphabet was meticulously designed by ancient grammarians like Pāṇini (पाणिनि), who classified sounds based on their place and manner of articulation. This scientific approach to phonetics is one of India's great contributions to linguistics.
