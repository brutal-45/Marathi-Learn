# Poetry (कविता)

Famous Marathi poems for advanced learners, featuring works from classical saint poets to modern contemporary poetry. Each poem includes analysis and interpretation.

## 1. Bahinabai Chaudhari - "Aai" (बहिणाबाई चौधरी - "आई")

### देवनागरी
**"आई"**

आई माझी गं माझ्या जिवाला ग,
किती सांगू तिच्या गुणांची ग वळण।
जशी माती तशी तिची मती ग,
सकळ सृष्टी तिचेच रूप ग असे।।

आई म्हणजे जगाचे आदार ग,
जगदाते तिचे नाव म्हणती।
लेकरु रडे तर दुःखी व्हावे ग,
झोपेतही ती त्यांची चिंता करे।।

अंगणात वावरे शेतात ती ग,
एक थांब न घेता दमत नाही।
परके वाटे तिचे सुख ग,
आणि स्वतःसाठी काही ठेवत नाही।।

बहिणाबाई म्हणे माझे मनीं ग,
आईची माया अवघ्या जगी।
तिच्या पायांखाली स्वर्ग आहे ग,
तिला नमस्कार मी विनवीतसे।।

### Transliteration
**"Āī"**

Āī mājhī ga mājhyā jīvālā ga,
Kitī sāṅgū ticyā guṇāncī ga vaḷaṇa.
Jaśī mātī taśī tichī matī ga,
Sakaḷ sṛṣṭī ticech rūpa ga ase.

Āī mhaṇaje jagāce ādāra ga,
Jagadāte tice nāva mhaṇatī.
Lekaru raḍe tara duḥkhī vhāve ga,
Jhopetahī tī tyañcī cintā kare.

Aṅgaṇāt vāvare śetāt tī ga,
Eka thāmba na ghetā damata nāhī.
Parake vāṭe tice sukha ga,
Āṇi svatahsāthī kāhī thevata nāhī.

Bahiṇābāī mhaṇe māje manīṃ ga,
Āīcī māyā avaghyā jagī.
Ticyā pāyāṅkhālī svarga āhe ga,
Tilā namaskāra mī vinavītase.

### English Translation
**"Mother"**

My mother, oh my life,
How much should I describe the nature of her qualities.
Like the earth, so is her mind,
The entire creation is her form.

Mother means the support of the world,
Creators call her by that name.
If a child cries, she becomes sad,
Even in sleep, she worries about them.

She moves in the courtyard, in the field,
Without stopping even once, she doesn't tire.
For others is her happiness,
And she keeps nothing for herself.

Bahinabai says in my heart,
Mother's love is in the entire world.
Beneath her feet is heaven,
I bow to her in prayer.

### Poetic Analysis

**About the Poet:** Bahinabai Chaudhari (1880-1951) was a renowned Marathi poetess known for her simple yet profound poems about rural life and family relationships.

**Form:** This poem uses "ग" (ga) as a filler word, characteristic of oral folk poetry from North Maharashtra (Khandesh region).

**Themes:**
- Mother's selfless love
- Comparison to Mother Earth
- Mother as the foundation of family and society

**Literary Devices:**
| Device | Marathi Example | Explanation |
|--------|-----------------|-------------|
| Upama (Simile) | जशी माती तशी तिची मती | Comparing mother's mind to earth |
| Anuprasa (Alliteration) | माती-मती | Sound repetition |
| Atashayokti (Hyperbole) | तिच्या पायांखाली स्वर्ग आहे | Heaven at her feet |

## 2. Kusumagraj - "Preeti" (कुसुमाग्रज - "प्रीती")

### देवनागरी
**"प्रीती"**

प्रीती हे नाव राधेचे,
नवल नाही तिचे ग माझे।
पण आज मी बोलतो,
प्रीती नाव माझेच ग माझे।।

प्रीती म्हणजे काय रे?
जगातले काही उत्तर नाही।
प्रीती म्हणजे रक्त म्हणजे प्राण,
पण कोणीच माप घालू शकत नाही।।

प्रीतीचा माग कोणी न सांगे,
ज्याला प्रीती त्याला वेड न सांगे।
वेडे ना बुद्धी ना बळ ना शक्ती,
प्रीती स्वतःच एक महाशक्ती।।

दिसता दिसत नाही प्रीती,
थांबता थांबत नाही प्रीती।
पाहता पाहत नाही ती दिसे,
पण सर्वत्र तीच असे वाटे।।

कुसुमाग्रज म्हणे हे सांगून,
प्रीतीचे मर्म कोणी जाणत नाही।
जगाच्या शेवटी जे उरेल,
ते प्रीतीचेच असे ठसे।।

### Transliteration
**"Prītī"**

Prītī he nāva rādheche,
Navala nāhī tiche ga māje.
Pan āja mī bolatō,
Prītī nāva mājech ga māje.

Prītī mhaṇaje kāya re?
Jagātale kāhī uttara nāhī.
Prītī mhaṇaje rakta mhaṇaje prāṇa,
Pan koṇīcha māpa ghālū śakata nāhī.

Prītīcā māga koṇī na sāṅge,
Jyālā prītī tyālā veḍa na sāṅge.
Veḍe nā buddhi nā baḷa nā śaktī,
Prītī svatahēc ek mahāśaktī.

Distā distata nāhī prītī,
Thāmbatā thāmbata nāhī prītī.
Pāhatā pāhata nāhī tī dise,
Pan sarvatra tīch ase vāṭe.

Kusumāgraj mhaṇe he sāṅgūna,
Prītīce marma koṇī jāṇata nāhī.
Jagācyā shevaṭī je urela,
Te prītīchech ase ṭhase.

### English Translation
**"Love"**

Love is Radha's name,
It's no wonder it's mine too.
But today I speak,
Love is my name, truly mine.

What is love, my friend?
There is no answer in the world.
Love means blood, means life,
But no one can measure it.

No one tells the origin of love,
To the one who loves, don't call them mad.
Neither madness, nor intellect, nor strength, nor power,
Love itself is a great power.

You see but don't see love,
You stop but love doesn't stop.
You look but don't see it appear,
But everywhere it seems to be present.

Kusumagraj says this much,
No one knows the secret of love.
What will remain at the end of the world,
Only love's mark will remain.

### Poetic Analysis

**About the Poet:** Kusumagraj (1912-1999), born Vishnu Vaman Shirwadkar, was a Jnanpith Award winner and one of the most influential modern Marathi poets.

**Form:** Modern Marathi poetry with philosophical undertones.

**Themes:**
- The ineffable nature of love
- Love as a cosmic force
- Love beyond reason and logic

## 3. Suresh Bhat - "Gazal" (सुरेश भाट - "गझल")

### देवनागरी
**"गझल"**

काय सांगू कशासाठी रडू, काय मागू कशासाठी जगू,
या जगाच्या मर्यादेत आज मी एकटाच राहिलो।

जोडलेली हातात निराशा, तीही आज फुटली,
जगातले सारे दुःख माझ्याच पाठीशी लागले।

माझे स्वप्न माझ्याच डोळ्यांत विरघून गेले,
माझी आशा माझ्याच मनात मावून गेली।

मला वाटे होते प्रेमाचा सागर पार करू,
पण पायाखाली वाळूच पसरली होती।

सुरेश भाट म्हणतात, हे जीवन कसे जगावे?
जेव्हा प्रेमाचे सूर्य मावळत चालले असतील।

### Transliteration
**"Gajala"**

Kāya sāṅgū kaśāsāthī raḍū, kāya māgū kaśāsāthī jagū,
Yā jagācyā maryādēt āja mī ekaṭāc rāhilō.

Joḍalī hātāta nirāśā, tīhī āja phuṭalī,
Jagātale sāre duḥkha mājhyāca pāṭhīśī lāgale.

Māje svapna mājhyāca ḍoḷyāta viraghūna gēle,
Mājī āśā mājhyāca manāta māvūna gēlī.

Malā vāṭe hōte premācā sāgara pāra karū,
Pan pāyākhālī vāḷūca pasaralī hōtī.

Surēṣa Bhāṭ mhaṇatāta, he jīvana kase jagāve?
Jevhā premāce sūrya māvaḷata cālalē asatīla.

### English Translation
**"Ghazal"**

What to say, why to cry, what to ask, why to live,
Within the boundaries of this world, I remained alone today.

Despair held in my hands, that too broke today,
All the sorrows of the world followed only me.

My dreams dissolved in my own eyes,
My hope suffocated within my own mind.

I thought I would cross the ocean of love,
But only sand was spread beneath my feet.

Suresh Bhat says, how to live this life?
When the suns of love are setting.

### Poetic Analysis

**About the Poet:** Suresh Bhat (1932-2003) was known as the "Ghazal Samrat" (Emperor of Ghazals) of Marathi poetry.

**Form:** Ghazal - a poetic form of Persian origin with couplets (sher) that share a common rhyme scheme.

**Themes:**
- Loneliness and despair
- Broken dreams
- The futility of love

## 4. Grace - "Matsyagandha" (ग्रेस - "मत्स्यगंधा")

### देवनागरी
**"मत्स्यगंधा" - अंश**

मी मत्स्यगंधा, माशांची वास असलेली,
पण माझे प्रेम सुगंधित आहे.
मी नाविकांची मुलगी,
किनाऱ्यावरच्या वाऱ्यात वाढलेली.

माझ्या नजरेत समुद्राची खोली आहे,
माझ्या डोळ्यांत लाटांची उंची आहे.
पावसाळी पाऊस वाळूत मिसळतो,
तसे माझे प्रेम तुझ्या अस्तित्वात मिसळेल.

मी साधी सरळ नाविक मुलगी,
मला तत्त्वज्ञान नाही आले.
पण प्रेमाचा अर्थ मला समजतो,
कारण प्रेम म्हणजे देणे आहे, घेणे नाही.

### Transliteration
**"Matsyagandhā" - Aṃśa**

Mī matsyagandhā, māśāncī vāsa asalelī,
Pan māje prēma sugandhita āhe.
Mī nāvikāncī mulagī,
Kināṛyāvarcyā vāṛyāta vāḍhalelī.

Mājhyā najarēta samudrācī kholī āhe,
Mājhyā ḍoḷyāta lāṭāncī uñcī āhe.
Pāvasāḷī pāusa vāḷūta misaḷatō,
Tase māje prēma tujhyā astitvāta misaḷēla.

Mī sādhī saraḷa nāvika mulagī,
Malā tattvajñāna nāhī āle.
Pan premācā artha malā samajatō,
Kāraṇa prēma mhaṇaje dēṇe āhe, ghēṇe nāhī.

### English Translation
**"Matsyagandha" - Excerpt**

I am Matsyagandha, with the smell of fish,
But my love is fragrant.
I am a boatman's daughter,
Grown in the winds on the shore.

In my gaze is the depth of the sea,
In my eyes is the height of waves.
Rainy season rain mixes in the sand,
So will my love mix in your existence.

I am a simple straightforward boatman's daughter,
I haven't learned philosophy.
But I understand the meaning of love,
Because love means giving, not taking.

### Poetic Analysis

**About the Poet:** Grace (1928-1993), born as Manik Godghate, was a poet known for his unique style and deeply emotional poetry.

**Themes:**
- Love across social boundaries
- Simplicity vs. complexity
- Selfless love

## 5. Mangesh Padgaonkar - "Salaam" (मंगेश पाडगावकर - "सलाम")

### देवनागरी
**"सलाम"**

सलाम! तुझ्या प्रेमाला मी सलाम!
जगात आलो मी तुझ्यासाठीच,
आणि जगातली सर्व सुखे माझ्या पायांखाली आणली.

तू नवल नाहीस का?
मी पाहिले तुझे चेहरे पाहून,
तुझ्या डोळ्यांत माझे स्वप्न पाहून.

सलाम! तुझ्या प्रेमाला मी सलाम!
ही जगण्याची वेळ, हे जगण्याचे त्राण,
हे सारे तुझ्यामुळेच आहे.

तू माझ्या जीवनात आलीस,
आणि माझे जीवन बदलले.
आता तू नाहीस तर काहीही नाही,
पण तू आहेस म्हणजे सारे आहे.

मंगेश म्हणतात, हे जीवन सुंदर आहे,
कारण तू या जीवनात आहेस.
तुझ्या प्रेमाला मी सलाम!

### Transliteration
**"Salāma"**

Salāma! Tujhyā premālā mī salāma!
Jagāta ālō mī tujhyāsāthīch,
Āṇi jagātalī sarva sukhē mājhyā pāyāṅkhālī āṇalī.

Tū navala nāhīsa kā?
Mī pāhile tujhe cehere pāhūna,
Tujhyā ḍoḷyāta māje svapna pāhūna.

Salāma! Tujhyā premālā mī salāma!
Hī jagaṇyācī veḷ, he jagaṇyāce trāṇa,
He sāre tujhyāmūḷech āhe.

Tū mājhyā jīvanāta ālis,
Āṇi māje jīvana badalale.
Ātā tū nāhīsa tar kāhīhī nāhī,
Pan tū āhesa mhaṇaje sāre āhe.

Maṅgeśa mhaṇatāta, he jīvana sundara āhe,
Kāraṇa tū yā jīvanāta āhes.
Tujhyā premālā mī salāma!

### English Translation
**"Salute"**

Salute! I salute your love!
I came into this world only for you,
And brought all the happiness in the world at my feet.

Aren't you a wonder?
I saw by looking at your face,
By seeing my dreams in your eyes.

Salute! I salute your love!
This time of living, this strength to live,
All this is because of you.

You came into my life,
And changed my life.
Now if you're not there, there's nothing,
But if you are, everything is.

Mangesh says, this life is beautiful,
Because you are in this life.
I salute your love!

### Poetic Analysis

**About the Poet:** Mangesh Padgaonkar (1929-2015) was a prolific poet, lyricist, and translator. His poems are known for their accessibility and emotional depth.

**Themes:**
- Romantic love
- Gratitude for beloved
- Life transformed by love

## Exercises (अभ्यास)

### Exercise 1: Reading Comprehension

Answer the following questions:
1. According to Bahinabai, how is mother similar to Mother Earth?
2. What does Kusumagraj say about the power of love?
3. Why does the poet in Suresh Bhat's ghazal feel alone?
4. What does Matsyagandha mean? What does it symbolize?
5. How does Mangesh Padgaonkar express gratitude in his poem?

### Exercise 2: Identify the Figure of Speech

Identify the literary device used:

1. "जशी माती तशी तिची मती" - (Upama/Simile)
2. "तिच्या पायांखाली स्वर्ग आहे" - (Atishayokti/Hyperbole)
3. "माझे स्वप्न माझ्याच डोळ्यांत विरघून गेले" - (Rupaka/Metaphor)
4. "प्रीती स्वतःच एक महाशक्ती" - (Rupaka/Metaphor)

### Exercise 3: Translate to Marathi

Translate these poetic expressions into Marathi:
1. Love is like a deep ocean.
2. My heart is full of hope.
3. The moon shines in the sky of my dreams.
4. Your eyes are like stars.
5. Life without love is like a garden without flowers.

### Exercise 4: Poetry Writing

Write a short poem (4-6 lines) in Marathi on one of these themes:
- निसर्ग (Nature)
- मैत्री (Friendship)
- आई (Mother)
- स्वप्न (Dreams)

### Exercise 5: Comparative Analysis

Compare two poems and discuss:
1. How is "love" depicted differently in Kusumagraj's and Mangesh Padgaonkar's poems?
2. Which poem do you relate to the most and why?

### Exercise 6: Recitation Practice

Practice reciting these poems with proper:
- Pronunciation
- Intonation (पारायण शैली)
- Emotional expression (भावनात्मक अभिव्यक्ती)
