# Classic Literature (प्राचीन साहित्य)

Excerpts from Marathi classical literature for advanced learners. These selections showcase the richness of Marathi literary tradition from saint poets to modern classics.

---

## 1. Saint Tukaram - Abhang (संत तुकाराम - अभंग)

### देवनागरी
**अभंग: "आतां शके ना देवा माझे बोलणे"**

आतां शके ना देवा माझे बोलणे।
अवघा जगाचा तूंचि कर्ता।।१।।

जैसा तैसा विठ्ठल माझा सांगे।
म्हणौनि मी विठ्ठल शब्दी रंगे।।२।।

तुझें माझें एकचि कांही।
बोलोनि काय सांडावें पाहीं।।३।।

जेथे जाईन तेथे तूंचि।
म्हणौनि मी जन्माचा पांगा न सोडिचि।।४।।

बोलतां बोलतां तोंड जाई।
सांगतां सांगतां श्रीधर भेटे।।५।।

तुका म्हणे विठ्ठल विठ्ठल करी।
पाहतां देव तुझेच रूप भारी।।६।।

### Transliteration
**Abhang: "Ātāṃ śake nā devā māje bolaṇe"**

Ātāṃ śake nā devā māje bolaṇe.
Avaghā jagāchā tūñchi kartā. (1)

Jaisā taisā viṭṭhal mājā sāṅge.
Mhaṇaunī mī viṭṭhal śabdī raṅge. (2)

Tujheṃ mājheṃ ekachi kāṃhī.
Bolonī kāya sāṇḍāveṃ pāhīṃ. (3)

Jehe jāīn tehe tūñchi.
Mhaṇaunī mī janmāchā pāṅgā na soḍici. (4)

Bolatā bolatā toṇḍa jāī.
Sāṅgatā sāṅgatā śrīdhara bheṭe. (5)

Tukā mhaṇe viṭṭhal viṭṭhal karī.
Pāhatā dev tujhech rūpa bhārī. (6)

### English Translation
**Abhang: "Now I cannot contain my speech, O God"**

Now I cannot contain my speech, O God.
You alone are the creator of the entire world. (1)

My Viththal speaks just as He pleases.
Therefore I am engrossed in the word 'Viththal'. (2)

Yours and mine - there is nothing but One.
What is there to say and leave unsaid? (3)

Wherever I go, there You are.
Therefore I do not abandon the attachment to life. (4)

Speaking and speaking, the mouth departs.
Narrating and narrating, the Lord meets. (5)

Tuka says, I keep saying Viththal, Viththal.
Looking at You, God, Your form is magnificent. (6)

### Literary Analysis (साहित्यिक विश्लेषण)

**Background:** Sant Tukaram (1608-1649) was a 17th-century Marathi poet-saint. His abhangs (devotional poems) form the foundation of the Warkari tradition.

**Themes:**
- Unity of the devotee and God
- The power of the divine name
- Complete surrender to God

**Key Concepts:**
| Marathi | Transliteration | Meaning |
|---------|-----------------|---------|
| अभंग | abhaṅga | Devotional poem (unbreakable) |
| कर्ता | kartā | Creator, doer |
| विठ्ठल | viṭṭhal | A form of Lord Krishna/Vishnu |
| शब्दी रंगणे | śabdī raṅgaṇe | To be engrossed in the word |
| पांगा न सोडणे | pāṅgā na soḍaṇe | Not to abandon attachment |
| भेटणे | bheṭaṇe | To meet |

## 2. Saint Dnyaneshwar - Pasaydan (संत ज्ञानेश्वर - पासायदन)

### देवनागरी
**पासायदन (अंश)**

पासायदन हें विनवीं दास विठ्ठला।
जगीं अवघें जन सुखी असावें।।

नाना विधांतरीं जे जे प्राणी विस्तारले।
ते ते विस्तरोनि अपार सुखी असावें।।

जे कोणीं सद्भक्तिरसें सेवितील।
ते निजानंदाचिये खेळीं रमतील।।

अंतरात्मया जोडोनियां परमात्मया।
देवासी जग वहावें म्हणौनिया।।

ज्ञानदेव म्हणे हें ग्रंथ जो पैं करी।
तो देवाचा विचारोनि पावसी श्रीहरी।।

### Transliteration
**Pāsāyadana (Aṃśa)**

Pāsāyadana heṃ vinavīṃ dāsa viṭṭhalā.
Jagīṃ avagheṃ jana sukhī asāveṃ. 

Nānā vidhāntarīṃ je je prāṇī vistārale.
Te te vistarōni apāra sukhī asāveṃ.

Je koṇīṃ sdbhaktirasēṃ sevitīla.
Te nijānandāciyē kheḷīṃ ramatīla.

Antarātmayā joḍoniyā paramātmayā.
Devāsī jaga vahāveṃ mhaṇauniyā.

Dnyānadēva mhaṇe heṃ grantha jo paiṃ karī.
To dēvācā vicārōni pāvasī śrīharī.

### English Translation
**Pasaydan (Benediction) - Excerpt**

This servant Viththal pleads for a boon:
May all people in the world be happy.

Whatever creatures have spread in various forms,
May they all be immensely happy.

Those who will serve with true devotion,
They will rejoice in the play of their own bliss.

May the world merge with God,
Connecting the individual soul with the Supreme Soul.

Dnyanadev says, whoever creates this scripture,
That person, through contemplation of God, will attain the Lord.

### Literary Analysis (साहित्यिक विश्लेषण)

**Background:** Sant Dnyaneshwar (1275-1296) wrote the Dnyaneshwari, a commentary on the Bhagavad Gita, at the age of 15. The Pasaydan is the concluding prayer of this great work.

**Themes:**
- Universal welfare and happiness
- Selfless prayer for others
- Devotion and spiritual knowledge

**Key Concepts:**
| Marathi | Transliteration | Meaning |
|---------|-----------------|---------|
| पासायदन | pāsāyadana | Benediction, boon |
| दास | dāsa | Servant, devotee |
| अवघे | avaghe | All, entire |
| सद्भक्तिरस | sdbhaktirasa | True devotional essence |
| निजानंद | nijānanda | Self-bliss |
| अंतरात्मा | antarātmā | Individual soul |
| परमात्मा | paramātmā | Supreme Soul |
| ग्रंथ | grantha | Scripture, book |

## 3. Keshavsut - Prem Mitra (केशवसुत - प्रेम मित्र)

### देवनागरी
**कविता: "प्रेम मित्र" - अंश**

सांग कोण तुझा सांगता येई?
ना तूं तत्त्व, ना अतीत कोणाचा!
ना दिसता येसी ना हात लागे,
फुलासारिखा सुगंध तुझा जणू!

जो कोणी जाणी तो जाणेच नाही,
तो अनुभवेच न वर्णन करी।
समुद्रापरी अथांग गंभीर,
आणि त्याच वेळी बालसुलभही!

### Transliteration
**Kavitā: "Prēma Mitra" - Aṃśa**

Sāṅga koṇ tujhā sāṅgatā yēī?
Nā tū tattva, nā atīta koṇāchā!
Nā distā yēsī nā hāta lāge,
Phulāsārikhā sugandha tujhā jaṇū!

Jo koṇī jāṇī to jāṇēcha nāhī,
To anubhavēcha na varṇana karī.
Samudrāparī athāṅga gambhīra,
Āṇi tyāch vēlī bālasulabhahī!

### English Translation
**Poem: "Love, My Friend" - Excerpt**

Tell me, who can describe you?
Neither are you a principle, nor do you belong to anyone's past!
Neither can you be seen, nor can you be touched,
Your presence is like the fragrance of a flower!

Whoever knows you, cannot explain,
They only experience, cannot describe.
Like the ocean, you are boundless and deep,
And at the same time, simple as a child!

### Literary Analysis (साहित्यिक विश्लेषण)

**Background:** Keshavsut (1866-1905), born Krishnaji Keshav Damle, is considered the father of modern Marathi poetry. He introduced new themes, meters, and sensibilities to Marathi literature.

**Themes:**
- The nature of love
- The paradox of experience vs. expression
- Depth and simplicity

**Key Concepts:**
| Marathi | Transliteration | Meaning |
|---------|-----------------|---------|
| तत्त्व | tattva | Principle, element |
| अतीत | atīta | Past |
| सुगंध | sugandha | Fragrance |
| अनुभव | anubhava | Experience |
| वर्णन | varṇana | Description |
| अथांग | athāṅga | Boundless, deep |
| गंभीर | gambhīra | Deep, serious |
| बालसुलभ | bālasulabha | Simple as a child |

## 4. PuLa Deshpande - Excerpt from "Batatyachi Chal" (पु.ल. देशपांडे - बटाट्याची चाळ)

### देवनागरी
**बटाट्याची चाळ - अंश**

मुंबईत चाळीचे जीवन वेगळेच असते. एका इमारतीत साठ-सत्तर कुटुंबे राहतात. प्रत्येकाची आपली गोष्ट, आपली समस्या, आपली सुख-दुःखे. पण चाळीत राहणाऱ्यांना एक गोष्ट समान असते - ती म्हणजे एकमेकांना ओळखणे.

आमच्या चाळीत अनेक रंगत लोक राहायचे. श्री. गोडबोले सकाळी सहा वाजता उठायचे आणि खूप मोठ्याने 'ॐ' म्हणायचे. त्यांच्या शेजारी राहणारे श्री. लेले म्हणायचे, "देवालाही नुसता डोळा लागू द्या गडे!"

संध्याकाळी सर्व महिला खाली गोळा होत. कोणाच्या मुलीचे लग्न, कोणाच्या मुलाचे नोकरीचे प्रश्न, कोणाची सासू आजारी - सगळ्यांच्या सगळ्या गोष्टी तिथे चर्चिल्या जात. ही चाळ म्हणजे एक लहान समाजाची प्रतिमा होती.

### Transliteration
**Baṭāṭyācī Cāḷ - Aṃśa**

Muṃbaīt cāḷīce jīvana vegḷeche asate. Ekā imārtīt sāṭha-sattara kuṭuṃbē rāhatāt. Pratyekācī āplī goshṭ, āplī samasyā, āplī sukh-duḥkhe. Pan cāḷīt rāhaṇāṟyānnā ek goshṭ samān asate - tī mhaṇaje ekamekānnā oḷakhaṇe.

Āmacyā cāḷīt aneka raṅgata lok rāhāyche. Shrī. Goḍabole sakālī sahā vājatā uṭhāyche āṇi khūp mōṭhyānē 'Ōṃ' mhaṇāyche. Tyāncyā shejārī rāhaṇāre Shrī. Lēle mhaṇāyche, "Dēvālāhī nustā ḍoḷyā lāgū dyā gaḍē!"

Saṃdhyākālī sarva mahilā khālī gōḷā hōt. Koṇācyā mulīce lagna, koṇācyā mulāce nōkarīce prashna, koṇācī sāsū ājārī - sagḷyāncyā sagḷyā goshṭī tithē charchilyā jāt. Hī cāḷ mhaṇaje ek lahan samājācī pratimā hotī.

### English Translation
**Batatyachi Chal - Excerpt**

Life in a Mumbai chawl (tenement) is unique. In one building, sixty-seventy families live. Each has their own story, their own problems, their own joys and sorrows. But one thing is common to all those living in the chawl - knowing each other.

In our chawl, many colorful people used to live. Mr. Godbole would wake up at six in the morning and chant 'Om' very loudly. His neighbor Mr. Lele would say, "At least let God just get some sleep, man!"

In the evening, all the women would gather downstairs. Someone's daughter's marriage, someone's son's job problems, someone's mother-in-law being sick - everyone's everything was discussed there. This chawl was like a miniature society.

### Literary Analysis (साहित्यिक विश्लेषण)

**Background:** Purushottam Laxman Deshpande (1919-2000), popularly known as PuLa, was a legendary Marathi writer, humorist, and performer. "Batatyachi Chal" (1955) is his famous one-man show describing life in a Mumbai chawl.

**Themes:**
- Community living in Mumbai
- Humor in everyday life
- Social commentary through wit

**Key Concepts:**
| Marathi | Transliteration | Meaning |
|---------|-----------------|---------|
| चाळ | cāḷ | Chawl, tenement |
| इमारत | imārata | Building |
| कुटुंब | kuṭuṃba | Family |
| समस्या | samasyā | Problem |
| सुख-दुःख | sukh-duḥkha | Joys and sorrows |
| ओळखणे | oḷakhaṇe | To know, recognize |
| रंगत | raṅgata | Colorful (character) |
| संध्याकाळी | saṃdhyākālī | In the evening |
| चर्चा | charchā | Discussion |
| प्रतिमा | pratimā | Image, reflection |

## 5. Vishnu Sakharam Khandekar - Yayati (वि.स. खांडेकर - ययाति)

### देवनागरी
**ययाति - अंश**

ययाति राजाने आपले तरुणपण पुत्राकडे दिले. पुर्विया राजाचे ऐकून देवांनी ययातिला स्वर्गात बोलावले. ययाति गर्वाने फुगला. त्याने इंद्राच्या आसनावर बसायचा प्रयत्न केला.

इंद्राने विचारले, "तू कोण?"
ययाति म्हणाला, "मी राजा ययाति. मी हजारो वर्षे जगलो. माझे तप अनंत आहे."

इंद्र म्हणाला, "हजारो वर्षे जगणाऱ्याने काय साधले? जो माणूस मरणाची वाट पाहत नाही, जो आपल्या इच्छा पूर्ण झाल्या असे समजतो, जो दुसऱ्याच्या सुखाचा विचार करीत नाही - असा मनुष्य अमर असला तरी मेल्यासारखाच आहे."

### Transliteration
**Yayāti - Aṃśa**

Yayāti rājāne āple taruṇapaṇa putrākaḍhe dile. Purviyā rājāche aikūn devānnī yayātilā svargāt bolāvale. Yayāti garvāne phugalā. Tyāne iṃdrācyā āsanāvara basāyachā prayatn kelā.

Iṃdrāne vichārle, "Tū koṇ?"
Yayāti mhaṇālā, "Mī rājā Yayāti. Mī hajārō varṣē jagalō. Māje tap ananta āhe."

Iṃdra mhaṇālā, "Hajārō varṣē jagaṇāṟyāne kāya sādhale? Jo māṇūsa maraṇācī vāṭa pāhat nāhī, jo āplyā ichchhā pūrṇa jhālyā ase samajato, jo dusryācyā sukhācā vicār karīta nāhī - asā manuṣya amara asalā tarī melyāsārakhāch āhe."

### English Translation
**Yayati - Excerpt**

King Yayati gave his youth to his son. Hearing of Purviya's sacrifice, the gods called Yayati to heaven. Yayati swelled with pride. He tried to sit on Indra's throne.

Indra asked, "Who are you?"
Yayati said, "I am King Yayati. I have lived for thousands of years. My penance is infinite."

Indra said, "What has one who has lived for thousands of years achieved? The person who does not await death, who thinks his desires are fulfilled, who does not think of others' happiness - such a person, even if immortal, is as good as dead."

### Literary Analysis (साहित्यिक विश्लेषण)

**Background:** V.S. Khandekar (1898-1976) won the Jnanpith Award for this novel "Yayati" (1959). It reinterprets the Mahabharata story of King Yayati.

**Themes:**
- Pride and its consequences
- The meaning of a meaningful life
- Selfishness vs. selflessness

**Key Concepts:**
| Marathi | Transliteration | Meaning |
|---------|-----------------|---------|
| ययाति | yayāti | King Yayati (mythological) |
| तरुणपण | taruṇapaṇa | Youth |
| स्वर्ग | svarga | Heaven |
| गर्व | garva | Pride |
| इंद्र | iṃdra | King of gods |
| तप | tap | Penance |
| अमर | amara | Immortal |
| मरण | maraṇa | Death |
| इच्छा | ichchhā | Desire |
