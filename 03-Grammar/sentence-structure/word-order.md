# Word Order in Marathi | मराठीतील शब्दक्रम

## Overview | सारांश

Marathi follows a **Subject-Object-Verb (SOV)** word order, which is different from English's Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) pattern. Understanding this fundamental difference is crucial for constructing grammatically correct Marathi sentences.

मराठी **कर्ता-कर्म-क्रियापद (SOV)** शब्दक्रम वापरते, जे इंग्रजीच्या Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) पॅटर्नपेक्षा वेगळे आहे. हा मूलभूत फरक समजून घेणे व्याकरणदृष्ट्या योग्य मराठी वाक्य बनवण्यासाठी अत्यंत आवश्यक आहे.

---

## Basic SOV Structure | मूळ SOV रचना

### Word Order Comparison | शब्दक्रम तुलना

| Language | Order | Example | Structure |
|----------|-------|---------|-----------|
| **English** | SVO | I eat rice | Subject → Verb → Object |
| **Marathi** | SOV | मी भात खातो | Subject → Object → Verb |
| **Transliteration** | - | mī bhāt khātō | mī (I) → bhāt (rice) → khātō (eat) |

### Sentence Pattern | वाक्य पॅटर्न

```
English: Subject + Verb + Object
Marathi: Subject + Object + Verb

English: I read a book
Marathi: मी पुस्तक वाचतो
(mī pustak vācato)
Literally: I book read
```

## Word Order Rules | शब्दक्रम नियम

### Rule 1: Verb at the End | नियम १: क्रियापद शेवटी

The verb **always** comes at the end of the main clause.

| English (SVO) | Marathi (SOV) | Transliteration |
|---------------|---------------|-----------------|
| I go to school | मी शाळेला जातो | mī śāḷelā jātō |
| She is happy | ती आनंदी आहे | tī ānandī āhe |
| They work hard | ते अधिक काम करतात | te adhik kām kartāt |

### Rule 2: Subject First | नियम २: कर्ता पहिला

The subject typically appears at the beginning of the sentence.

| English | Marathi | Transliteration |
|---------|---------|-----------------|
| The boy plays | मुलगा खेळतो | mulgā kheḷatō |
| My mother cooks | माझी आई स्वयंपाक करते | mājhī āī svayaṃpāk karte |
| Students study | विद्यार्थी अभ्यास करतात | vidyārthī abhyās kartāt |

### Rule 3: Object Before Verb | नियम ३: कर्म क्रियापदापूर्वी

The object comes before the verb.

| English | Marathi | Transliteration |
|---------|---------|-----------------|
| I see a bird | मी पक्षी पाहतो | mī pakṣī pāhato |
| She loves music | ती संगीत आवडते | tī saṅgīt āvaḍate |
| He teaches children | तो मुलांना शिकवतो | tō mulāṃnā śikavato |

### Rule 4: Postpositions After Nouns | नियम ४: शब्दयोगी अव्यय नामांनंतर

Postpositions (like English prepositions) come **after** the noun they modify.

| English (Preposition) | Marathi (Postposition) | Transliteration |
|-----------------------|------------------------|-----------------|
| in the house | घरात | gharāt |
| on the table | टेबलवर | ṭebalavar |
| with friends | मित्रांसोबत | mitrāṃsobat |
| from Pune | पुण्याहून | puṇyāhūn |

---

## Word Order with Postpositions | शब्दयोगी अव्ययांसह शब्दक्रम

### Complete Sentence Structure | पूर्ण वाक्य रचना

```
Subject + [Object + Postposition] + Verb

English: I go to school
Marathi: मी शाळेला जातो
Breakdown: मी (I) + शाळेला (to school) + जातो (go)

English: She talks with friends
Marathi: ती मित्रांशी बोलते
Breakdown: ती (She) + मित्रांशी (with friends) + बोलते (talks)
```

### Examples with Different Postpositions | विविध शब्दयोगी अव्ययांसह उदाहरणे

| English | Marathi | Transliteration | Postposition |
|---------|---------|-----------------|--------------|
| I live in Mumbai | मी मुंबईत राहतो | mī muṃbaīt rāhato | -त (in) |
| The book is on the table | पुस्तक टेबलवर आहे | pustak ṭebalavar āhe | -वर (on) |
| She comes from Delhi | ती दिल्लीहून येते | tī dillīhūn yete | -हून (from) |
| He works for the company | तो कंपनीसाठी काम करतो | tō kampaṇīsāṭhī kām karto | -साठी (for) |

## Flexible Word Order for Emphasis | अधोरेखनासाठी लवचिक शब्दक्रम

While SOV is the standard order, Marathi allows flexibility to emphasize different parts of a sentence.

SOV हा मानक क्रम असला तरी, वाक्याच्या विविध भागांवर अधोरेखन देण्यासाठी मराठी लवचिकता दाखवते.

### Standard Order | मानक क्रम

| Sentence | Emphasis | Meaning |
|----------|----------|---------|
| मी आज बाजारात जातो | Neutral | I go to the market today |
| (mī āj bājārāt jātō) | | Standard statement |

### Emphasizing the Subject | कर्त्यावर अधोरेखन

| Sentence | Emphasis | Meaning |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **मी** आज बाजारात जातो | **I** go to the market today | Not someone else |
| (emphasis on मी) | | |

### Emphasizing the Object/Place | कर्म/स्थानावर अधोरेखन

| Sentence | Emphasis | Meaning |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **बाजारात** मी आज जातो | To the **market** I go today | Not elsewhere |
| (emphasis on बाजारात) | | |

### Emphasizing Time | काळावर अधोरेखन

| Sentence | Emphasis | Meaning |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **आज** मी बाजारात जातो | **Today** I go to the market | Not another day |
| (emphasis on आज) | | |

### Comparison Table | तुलना सारणी

| Word Order | Marathi | Transliteration | Emphasis |
|------------|---------|-----------------|----------|
| Standard SOV | राम आंबा खातो | rām āṃbā khātō | Neutral: Ram eats mango |
| Object fronted | आंबा राम खातो | āṃbā rām khātō | Focus on mango (not other fruit) |
| With time word | राम आज आंबा खातो | rām āj āṃbā khātō | Ram eats mango today |
| Time fronted | आज राम आंबा खातो | āj rām āṃbā khātō | TODAY Ram eats mango |

## Comparison with English | इंग्रजीशी तुलना

### Side-by-Side Structure Comparison | रचनेची बाजूने तुलना

| Feature | English (SVO) | Marathi (SOV) |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| **Basic Order** | I eat rice | मी भात खातो (I rice eat) |
| **Prepositions/Postpositions** | in the house | घरात (house-in) |
| **Adjective Position** | big house | मोठे घर (big house - same) |
| **Adverb Position** | He speaks well | तो चांगला बोलतो (He well speaks) |
| **Time Words** | I go today | मी आज जातो (I today go) |

### Complex Sentence Comparison | जटिल वाक्य तुलना

| English | Marathi | Transliteration |
|---------|---------|-----------------|

| They will come to our house tomorrow | ते उद्या आमच्या घरी येतील | te udyā āmacyā gharī yetīl |

## Key Takeaways | मुख्य मुद्दे

1. **Verb at End**: In Marathi, the verb always comes at the end of the sentence.
2. **SOV Pattern**: Subject-Object-Verb is the standard word order.
3. **Postpositions**: Unlike English prepositions, Marathi uses postpositions that come after nouns.
4. **Flexible Order**: Word order can change for emphasis, but the verb stays at the end.
5. **Practice**: Regular practice helps internalize the different word order pattern.
